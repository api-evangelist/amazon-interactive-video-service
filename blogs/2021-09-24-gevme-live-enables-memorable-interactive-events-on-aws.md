<p>In 2015 <a href="https://www.globalsignin.com/">GlobalSign.in</a>, an event technology company, developed <a href="https://www.gevme.com/en/">GEVME</a>, a technology platform providing end-to-end solutions for managing virtual, face-to-face, or hybrid meetings and conferences. With in-person meetings and conferences canceled in 2020, the company wanted to build on its existing platform to offer fully customizable digital venues with a focus on immersive and engaging experiences.</p> 
<p>With the agility of AWS, <a href="http://GlobalSign.in">GlobalSign.in</a> quickly built on GEVME to produce GEVME Live. The company used <a href="https://aws.amazon.com/ivs/">Amazon Interactive Video Service</a> (Amazon IVS) to create an unlimited number of simultaneous live events, and <a href="https://aws.amazon.com/chime/">Amazon Chime</a> for participant interaction in breakout sessions, watch parties, and chat rooms. <a href="https://aws.amazon.com/solutions/case-studies/gevme/"><b>Read the full case study</b></a> to learn how GEVME Live uses AWS services to provide low-latency, high-quality live events while ensuring data security and compliance with encryption and logging best practices.</p> 
<p>AWS offers the most purpose-built services for direct-to-consumer (D2C) and streaming to help companies reliably deliver, monetize, and support live and on-demand media over the internet, and bring unparalleled experiences to screens everywhere. Explore <a href="https://aws.amazon.com/media/direct-to-consumer-d2c-streaming/">https://aws.amazon.com/media/direct-to-consumer-d2c-streaming/</a> to learn more.</p>
